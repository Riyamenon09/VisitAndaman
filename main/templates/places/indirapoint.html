Indira Point — Great Nicobar

Overview

Indira Point is the southernmost tip of India, located on Great Nicobar Island. It is marked by a tall lighthouse standing against the vast Indian Ocean. This remote site is ecologically sensitive and strategically significant.

  • Region: Great Nicobar (Nicobar Islands)
  • Feature: Lighthouse built in 1972
  • Vibe: Remote, wild, far from tourist routes

Access & Permits

Indira Point is not freely open to tourists. Visitors require special permits issued by the administration, and travel involves reaching Campbell Bay by ship or helicopter, followed by road journey southwards.

  • Advance government permits required
  • Travel restricted for ecological and security reasons
  • Best season: Nov–Apr (calm seas)

Travel is subject to change based on weather and regulations — always confirm before planning.

Highlights

  • The Indira Point Lighthouse at the edge of the ocean.
  • Dramatic views of the Ten Degree Channel.
  • Wild untouched landscapes of Great Nicobar.

Responsible Travel

  • Follow all rules laid down by local authorities and the Indian Navy.
  • No littering; carry back all waste.
  • Respect the sensitive tribal & ecological zones of Great Nicobar.
