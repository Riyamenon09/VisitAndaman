Ross Island — Ruins & Colonial Heritage

Overview

Ross Island (officially Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Island) was once the administrative headquarters of the British. Known as the “Paris of the East,” it housed opulent bungalows, a church, a bakery and clubs. Today, banyan roots embrace the crumbling walls, creating one of the most atmospheric historic sites in the Andamans.

  • Ferry: 10–15 min from Port Blair Water Sports Complex
  • Time needed: 2–3 hours
  • Best time: Oct–Apr; morning ferries are nicest

Highlights

  • Ruins of the church, bakery, hospital and officers’ quarters
  • Japanese bunkers from the World War II occupation
  • Deer park and shaded banyan avenues—easy walking circuit
  • Evening Sound & Light Show narrating the island’s past

Visitor Info

  • Open: ~8:30 AM – 5 PM (often closed on Wed; confirm locally)
  • Tickets: Island entry + ferry ticket at Water Sports Complex
  • Facilities are limited—carry water and light snacks
