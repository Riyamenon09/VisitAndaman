Havelock Island — Beaches, Diving & Slow Life

Overview

Havelock Island (officially Swaraj Dweep) is the Andamans’ poster child—powdery white sands, lagoons in shades of turquoise, coral reefs, and a relaxed island vibe. It’s India’s scuba capital and a honeymoon favorite, yet still feels laid-back once you leave the jetty road.

  • Ferry: ~2–2.5 hrs from Port Blair; ~1–1.5 hrs from Neil
  • Stay: Beach huts to luxury resorts along Beach No. 5 & 2
  • Best time: Oct–Apr (driest seas, best visibility)

Must-Visit Beaches

  • Radhanagar Beach (Beach No. 7): Often listed among Asia’s best—broad sugar-white sands, gentle gradient, spectacular sunsets. No water sports here, it’s kept pristine.
  • Elephant Beach: Reach by 20-min speedboat or a 30–40 min forest trek; best for snorkeling and water activities.
  • Kalapathar Beach: A moody sunrise spot with black rocks and casuarina fringes—great for photos.
  • Vijaynagar / Govindnagar (Beach 5 & 3): Lagoon-like shallows with many beachfront stays.

Diving & Watersports

Havelock has India’s most reliable dive training and reef access. For first-timers, try a Discover Scuba with certified centers; for certified divers, signature sites include Aquarium, The Wall, Barracuda City, and Johnny’s Gorge. Expect turtles, rays, schooling snappers, and healthy hard-coral gardens.

  • Other activities: Kayaking (including bioluminescence nights), SUP, glass-bottom boat, sea walk at Elephant Beach
  • Best viz: Dec–Mar; shoulder season Oct/Apr is pleasant
  • Always choose SSI/PADI certified operators

Suggested 2–3 Day Itinerary

  • Day 1: Ferry to Havelock → check-in → evening at Radhanagar (sunset).
  • Day 2: Morning scuba (or DSD). Afternoon rest. Late-evening bioluminescence kayak (on moonless nights).
  • Day 3: Boat to Elephant Beach for snorkeling & water sports → lunch near jetty → Kalapathar for sunrise/souvenirs → depart.

Staying & Eating

Most accommodations line the east coast road (Beach 5/3). Expect cafés serving fresh seafood, Indian and continental fare. Beach shacks close early; plan dinners by 9–9:30 PM.

  • Budget beach huts → boutique eco-resorts → luxury villas
  • Try grilled fish, coconut curries, and fresh juices
  • ATMs limited; many places accept UPI/cards but carry some cash

Visitor Info

  • Getting there: Govt/private ferries from Port Blair & Neil (book early in season).
  • Local transport: Rent scooters/bicycles; taxis available. Roads are simple—drive slow at night.
  • Connectivity: Mobile data works but can be spotty; many cafés offer Wi-Fi.
  • Responsible travel: No coral/sea-shell collection; avoid stepping on reefs at low tide.

Getting There

High-speed ferries run daily from Port Blair’s Haddo/PHR jetties and from Neil Island. On arrival at Havelock Jetty, autos and rentals are available. Pre-book tickets in peak months (Dec–Feb).

Tips

  • Sun is strong—carry reef-safe sunscreen, hat, and plenty of water.
  • For bioluminescence, pick moonless nights; ask operators for slots.
  • Respect beach flags; currents can change with tides/season.
  • Book diving with certified SSI/PADI centers; avoid touching corals/marine life.
