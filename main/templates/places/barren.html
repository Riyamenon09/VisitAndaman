Barren Island — India’s Active Volcano

Overview

Barren Island, about 135 km northeast of Port Blair, is the site of South Asia’s only confirmed active volcano. Rising dramatically from the Bay of Bengal, the island is uninhabited except for occasional monitoring teams. Tourists are not allowed to land — you can only view from a vessel or join liveaboard scuba expeditions in the surrounding waters.

  • First eruption recorded: 1787; most recent: 2017–18
  • Uninhabited; protected area
  • Accessible only by sea (charter/ferry/liveaboard)

Highlights

  • Black volcanic slopes with smoking vents (when active).
  • Stark, lunar-like landscape with no permanent vegetation on the crater.
  • Pristine reefs and clear waters surrounding the island.
  • Rare chance to witness an active volcano in Indian territory.

Diving Experience

The waters around Barren Island are considered some of the best in the world. Advanced divers on liveaboard boats can explore lava-formed reefs with 30+ meter visibility, encountering manta rays, reef sharks, schooling barracuda, tunas, and large pelagics.

  • Top dive sites: Lava Flow, Purple Haze, Manta Point
  • Best season: Nov–Apr (calm seas, best visibility)
  • Requires: Advanced Open Water certification recommended

Visitor Info

  • Access: Day boats from Havelock/Port Blair (view only) OR liveaboard diving trips.
  • Permits: Mandatory, arranged via operators.
  • Stay: No accommodation — overnight only on vessels.
  • Duration: 12–14 hrs for day boat, 3–6 nights for liveaboard expeditions.

Safety & Eco Guidelines

  • Landing on the island is prohibited to protect fragile ecosystems.
  • Trips are weather-dependent — always check forecast before booking.
  • Scuba diving only with authorized, certified operators.
  • Carry seasickness medication if prone; waters can get choppy.

Tips

  • Photographers: Best time is morning light; carry zoom lens for crater shots from boat.
  • Divers: Expect strong currents — plan with experienced dive leaders.
  • Pack: Water, snacks, sunscreen, hat, dry bag (for day boats).
