Baratang Limestone Caves

Overview

A classic Andaman day trip, Baratang combines an early-morning drive on the Andaman Trunk Road (ATR), a scenic mangrove creek boat ride and a short forest walk to dazzling stalactite–stalagmite formations. Great for families and first-timers.

  • Distance from Port Blair: ~100 km (start very early)
  • Journey pieces: ATR convoy → Middle Strait Jetty → speedboat to Nayadera → 1 km walk
  • Ideal months: Nov–Apr (calmer seas/less rain)

Getting There & Timings

  • Start: Hotels pick-up typically between 3:30–4:30 AM.
  • Convoy: Drive on the ATR in designated slots to Middle Strait Jetty.
  • Boat: Shared speedboats (life jackets provided) through mangroves to Nayadera jetty.
  • Walk: ~1 km easy trail to the caves (mixed boardwalk/soil path).
  • Total time: 10–12 hours round-trip from Port Blair (traffic/queue dependent).

What You’ll See

  • Limestone Caverns: Dripping stalactites, rising stalagmites, pillars and flowstone.
  • Mangrove Channels: Tunnels of roots and pneumatophores—great photo ops.
  • Forest Trail: Tropical understory, occasional birds/reptiles; shaded and level most of the way.

Tickets, Costs & Permits

  • Boat ticket: Shared speedboat per person (buy at jetty/through tour operator).
  • Cave entry: Nominal fee at checkpost (cash/UPI as available).
  • Package tours: Many operators bundle transfers + boat + guide; compare inclusions.
  • ID: Carry government ID; convoy/jetty checks are routine.

Note: Prices change by season; confirm a day prior with your hotel/operator.

Sample Day Plan

  • 03:45 Pickup → ATR convoy to Middle Strait
  • 07:30 Boat to Nayadera → forest walk → explore caves
  • 10:30 Return boat → snack break at jetty
  • 11:30 Optional: Mud Volcano side-trip
  • 13:30 Start back to Port Blair (arrive ~15:30–16:30)

Safety & Eco Guidelines

  • Wear closed shoes; rocks can be wet/slippery. Keep hands free (backpack over shoulder bag).
  • Do not touch/knock formations—stalactites are fragile and take centuries to form.
  • Follow boat/forest staff; stay on marked paths. Dispose of waste properly.
  • Carry water and light snacks; facilities are basic.

Nearby Add-Ons

  • Mud Volcano: 30–45 min stop; bubbling mud domes (geological curiosity).
  • Parrot Island (evening, Baratang): Boat at dusk to see thousands of parrots return to roost (weather/availability dependent).

What to Carry

  • Water (1–2 L), snacks, hat, sunscreen, mosquito repellent.
  • Closed shoes/sandals with grip; light rain jacket in shoulder months.
  • Small torch/phone light for cave nooks (guides also carry lights).

FAQ

  • Fitness required? Basic. The walk is short and mostly level.
  • Kids/elderly friendly? Yes with supervision; avoid rush hours/heat if sensitive.
  • Food/toilets? Available at jetty stalls; options are simple.
  • Photography? Allowed; keep flash minimal inside to preserve fauna.
