Flag Point — Where Netaji Hoisted the Tricolour

Overview

Flag Point in Port Blair marks the historic spot where Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose hoisted the Indian tricolour on 30th December 1943, declaring Andaman & Nicobar as freed territory from British rule. Today, the site features a tall flag mast, memorial plaques, and a peaceful promenade along the seafront.

  • Located: South Point, Port Blair (~10 mins from city centre)
  • Best time: Evening (sunset views over the harbour)
  • Time needed: 20–30 mins

Historic Significance

On 30th December 1943, during World War II, Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose, as the leader of the Azad Hind Government, hoisted the tricolour here to mark India’s first symbolic claim of freedom from colonial rule. He renamed the islands:

  • Andaman → Shaheed Dweep
  • Nicobar → Swaraj Dweep

Today, the spot is maintained by the Indian Navy and stands as a reminder of Netaji’s vision and sacrifices.

Highlights

  • Tall tricolour flag mast fluttering against the sea backdrop.
  • Commemorative plaques with details of Netaji’s visit.
  • Quiet promenade with benches and sea breeze.
  • Beautiful sunset photography point overlooking Ross Island in the distance.

Visitor Info

  • Location: South Point, Port Blair
  • Entry: Free, open to all
  • Timings: Open all day; evenings most popular
  • Best combined with: Marina Park, Cellular Jail light & sound show
