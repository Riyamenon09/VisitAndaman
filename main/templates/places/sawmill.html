Chatham Saw Mill — Asia’s Oldest Timber Mill

← Back

Overview

The Chatham Saw Mill, established in 1883, is among Asia’s oldest and largest timber mills. Located on Chatham Island and connected to Port Blair by a bridge, it showcases the colonial timber industry and the natural wealth of the Andamans.

  • ~10 minutes drive from Aberdeen Bazaar
  • Still operational in limited capacity
  • On-site Forest Museum for visitors

History

Built by the British to process rich hardwoods like teak, padauk, and satinwood, the mill supplied timber across South & Southeast Asia. During World War II, Chatham Island was bombed by Japanese forces, leaving marks still visible. Post-independence, it has served both industrial and heritage roles.

Highlights

  • Saw Mill Machinery: Massive bandsaws and log yards from colonial times.
  • Forest Museum: Exhibits on Andaman forestry, rare timber samples, dioramas of wildlife, and handicrafts.
  • WWII History: Sections of the mill still show bomb damage from 1942.
  • Bridge Views: Entry via Chatham Bridge offers views of Port Blair’s harbor.

Visitor Info

  • Location: Chatham Island, Port Blair (connected by road bridge).
  • Entry Fee: Nominal charge (~₹20–50; camera fee extra).
  • Timings: ~8:30 AM – 2:30 PM (Mon–Sat, closed Sundays/holidays).
  • Time Needed: 1–2 hours (mill tour + museum).
