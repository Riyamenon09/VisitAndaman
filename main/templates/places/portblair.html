Geography & Climate

Port Blair sits on South Andaman Island facing the Bay of Bengal. The coastline has serene coves and mangroves, while low hills are cloaked in tropical evergreen forest. Weather is warm and maritime year-round; the clearest seas for snorkeling/diving are from October to April.

Heritage & Historical Life

From indigenous settlements to the British penal colony and the freedom struggle, Port Blair’s past centers around the Cellular Jail—now a National Memorial—and the ruins on Ross (Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Island) and Viper Islands.

Culture, Markets & Local Life

A friendly island mosaic—Bengali, Tamil, Telugu and Nicobari influences—visible in festivals, food, and crafts. Explore Aberdeen Bazaar for shells and spices, and enjoy folk dances during the Island Tourism Festival.

Sports & Activities

Adventure options include scuba diving and snorkeling at North Bay/Jolly Buoy, kayaking through mangroves, jet-skiing at Corbyn’s Cove (weather-permitting), and short treks like Munda Pahad at Chidiya Tapu.
